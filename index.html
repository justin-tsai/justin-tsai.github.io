About Me

I'm Justin, and I recently graduated Cal Poly Pomona in May with a Bachelors Degree in Computer Science. I describe myself as a hard worker and passionate learner that always seeks to tackle on new challenges and learn new skills. I have four years of Java experience and two years of Unity and C# experience. I worked at the Cal Poly Pomona Virtual Reality Lab to research ways VR can be incorporated into research and education, eventually taking over lab's operations as an adminstrator. As administrator, I was responsible for leading and managing the development of unique VR and AR projects using Unity. I mainly work with SteamVR and VRTK to develop for virtual reality, and Vuforia/ARCore to develop for augmented reality. Currently I work for my university as a Unity Engineer, developing unique VR experiences for clients in the education domain. Let me know if you want to work together!

Projects

Block ARhythm

Block ARhythm is a rhythm game with a twist: notes not only come from the front, but the sides as well. The player controls the colored barriers and use them to block the notes incoming from all sides from hitting the center cube, but only one barrier can be active at a time. The game can also be played in Augmented Reality on the phone.

Tools: Unity, C#, Visual Studio, Vuforia

Spellbreaker VR

Spellbreaker VR is a game made in Unity. Set in a cosmic space-like environment, the player uses swords to slash through a flurry of magic projectiles aimed at them. This is my take on Beat Saber, but with a small twist of being in a different environment and added mechanics. I was responsible for game design, mechanics, audio, particle systems, and player controls.

Tools: Unity, C#, Visual Studio, SteamVR, HTC Vive

VR Combination and Permutation

VR Combination and Permutation is a research project that aims to test the player's understanding of combination and permutation by having them solve puzzles using each concept to escape a trapped room. Each puzzle has statistics to display the number of possible guesses to the solution. The project was presented to Cal Poly Pomona's 13th Annual Science Research Symposium. I was responsible for environment interactions, audio, and player controls.

Tools: Unity, C#, Visual Studio, MonoDevelop, VRTK, Oculus Rift

Below Haven

Below Haven is a project I developed in a team of 5 that made for my game development class. The idea for the game was heavily inspired by the series Made In Abyss. This is a 2D game in which the player has to travel underground to discover treasure and riches, but the levels and enemies get harder as they venture further below. I found assets and implemented the animations and scripts for player controls, UI, and audio.
